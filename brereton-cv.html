Andrew E. BreretonÂ¶

I’m a formally trained structural biologist, with a strong emphasis on tool development and data science techniques. The focus of my research thus far has been to better understand and describe the details of protein geometry and structural dynamics. To that end I’ve developed software called The Ensemblator (github.com/atomoton/ensemblator), a Python application that lets a user build, compare, analyze, and/or automatically cluster large sets of protein structures. From my experience working with protein structural data, I’ve gained a familiarity and appreciation for working with large, sparse, noisy datasets, and a familiarity with techniques for classifying and analyzing such sparse biological data. Data Science and especially dimensionality reduction techniques allow for the visualization of patterns that are too complex to normally observe, and a long-term interest I have is to apply these tools toward the understanding of intelligence and the brain, both in its disorders and it’s healthy functioning; I would especially love to work toward the sci-fi dream of truly connecting humans to machines using brain-computer interfaces, and believe that this problem will require a holistic understanding of the brain, and a huge collaborative effort, which includes the application of data science techniques to complex neurological data.

Software Development ProjectsÂ¶

  • The Ensemblator

    Python program to generate and analyze ensembles of protein structures. Uses a novel “core” based method for determining optimal structural overlays. Groups for comparison can be manually defined, or automatically detected using clustering. Capable of comparing ensembles of non-identical proteins, using sequence alignment before structural overlay.

  • The Reassigner

    Python program capable of assigning secondary structure to a pdb formatted protein structure. Assigns by using a combination of results from machine learning assignments of secondary structure based on local conformation, and a calculation of a virtual geometry. Capable of a very large range of intelligent secondary structure assignments.

  • Uncomplicated I Ching

    A minimal android app to enable consulting the I Ching Oracle.
